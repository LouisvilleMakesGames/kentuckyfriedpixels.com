What is Kentucky Fried Pixels?

Kentucky Fried Pixels is a month-long series of events designed to empower and nurture local game creators and provide them the tools necessary to successfully complete a game from initial concept to polish, marketing, release, and beyond.


What is a game jam?

A game jam is a hackathon for video games. It is a gathering of people for the purpose of planning, designing, and creating one or more games within a short span of time, usually ranging between 24 and 72 hours, and some even longer. Kentucky Fried Pixels lasts a full month from August 2 – September 1. All events throughout the month will be held at Warp Zone Louisville – our dedicated space for game development in downtown Louisville, KY.


Who can participate?

Anyone! Teams are made up of developers, 2D and 3D artists, storytellers, musicians, designers, and anyone who is interested in learning more about making games.


Where does the jam take place?


Warp Zone Louisville

612 West Washington St.
Louisville, KY 40202

What does it cost?

Absolutely nothing. All Louisville Makes Games events are free, welcoming to all skill levels, and open to the public. We are able to do this with funding from co-working members, and generous donations from members of the community who want to support local creatives just like you!
